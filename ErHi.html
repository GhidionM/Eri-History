Learn About Eritrean History

Eritrea is a country located in the Horn of Africa, bordered by Sudan to the west, Ethiopia to the south, and Djibouti to the southeast.
It has a long coastline along the Red Sea to the east. Here are some key points about Eritrea: History Ancient and Medieval History: Eritrea has a rich history with influences from various civilizations, including the ancient Kingdom of Aksum. The area has been inhabited for thousands of years, with evidence of ancient trade routes.
Colonial Era: Eritrea was colonized by Italy in the late 19th century and became a part of Italian East Africa.
After World War II, it was placed under British administration. Union with Ethiopia: In 1962, Eritrea was annexed by Ethiopia, leading to a long struggle for independence. Independence: Eritrea gained independence from Ethiopia in 1993 after a 30-year armed struggle, becoming an independent nation.
